Instructions

This experiment is an interactive two player game of chance. The players cooperate to place bets on the content of an urn.

Here's some examples of urns:

Both players know that the urn always contains 10 balls of one or more colors.

One player (the sender) is allowed to look inside the urn. But he or she cannot place bets.

Another player (the receiver) can ask one question to the sender before placing his or her bet.

When you are ready to begin, please answer the following question and click on the button below.

How many balls are there in the urn?
Correct!
